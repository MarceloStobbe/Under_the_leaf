Organic Homemade Fertilizers

Nourishing your plants doesn't require expensive synthetic chemicals. Many common household items are packed with the essential nutrients—Nitrogen, Phosphorus, and Potassium—that your green companions need to thrive. Using organic, homemade fertilizers is a sustainable way to recycle waste while providing a gentle, steady supply of nutrients, ensuring your soil remains healthy and your plants grow vibrant and strong.

1. Eggshell Calcium Boost

Eggshells are a fantastic source of calcium, which helps strengthen plant cell walls and prevent blossom end rot. To prepare this, simply collect your used eggshells, rinse them thoroughly, and let them dry completely. Once dry, crush them into a fine powder using a mortar and pestle or a blender. Sprinkle this powder directly onto the topsoil around your plants. This slow-release supplement is perfect for tomatoes, peppers, and leafy greens.

2. Banana Peel Potassium Tea

Bananas are rich in potassium, a vital nutrient that promotes flowering and overall plant vitality. Making a "banana tea" is an incredibly simple process: place fresh banana peels in a jar, cover them with water, and let them soak for 24 to 48 hours. Remove the peels and use the nutrient-rich water to feed your plants. This liquid fertilizer is ideal for indoor potted plants and flowering shrubs that need an extra boost during their growth cycle.
